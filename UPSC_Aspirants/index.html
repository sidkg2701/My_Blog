Indian Administrative Service (IAS)

Indian Administrative Service is one of the 3 All India Services.
IAS is the permanent arm of the Government of India and State Governments.
IAS cadre is responsible for formulating and implementing Government policies.
The Indian Administrative Service (IAS) is an All India Administrative Civil Service of India.
The IAS probationers begin their training at LBSNAA, Mussoorie.
The Indian Administrative Service is the most popular civil service among all the aspiring candidates.

Indian Police Service (IPS)

Indian Police Service is the one among the three All India Services.
IPS officers are trained at Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel National Police Academy in Hyderabad.
The IPS officers occupy senior positions in Police service.
The IPS officers occupy senior positions in RAW, IB, CBI etc.

Indian Foreign Service (IFoS)

IFS officers begin their training at LBSNAA and then it moves on to Foreign Service Institute located in New Delhi.
It is one of the most popular Group ‘A’ civil service.
IFS officers handle the Foreign affairs of India.
IFS officers can go on to become High Commissioner, Ambassadors, Permanent Representative of India at UN, and Foreign Secretary.
A candidate selected into IFS cannot reappear for the Civil Service Exam.

Indian Railway Traffic Service (IRTS)

IRTS comes under Group ‘A’ civil service.
IRTS cadre officers undergo training at Railway Staff College located at Vadodara and Indian Railway Institute of Transportation Management located in Lucknow.
Their primary responsibility of revenue generation for Indian Railways.
This service acts as the bridge between Railways and public; railways and corporate sector.
